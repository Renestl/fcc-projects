Charles R. Drew

Saving lives through blood banking

Drawing of Dr. Charles Richard Drew, the life-saving doctor and athlete

Read about Dr. Drew below:

  • 1904 - Born in Washington, DC.
  • 1922 - Leaves for Amherst College in Massachusetts.
  • 1933 - Recieved MDCM (Medicinae Doctor et Chirurgiae Magister, which means "doctor of medicine and master of surgery) from McGill University in Montreal, Canada.
  • 1938 - Accepted research fellowhip from the Rockefeller Foundation to study blood at New York’s Columbia – Presbyterian Medical Center
  • 1938 to 1940? - Became first African American to earn Doctor of Medical Science degree (equivalent to Ph.D.)
  • 1940 - Set up and administered early prototype program for blood storage and preservation, under the name 'Plasma for Britian'.
  • 1940 to 1941 - American Red Cross begas National Blood Donor Service based on Plasma for Britian.
  • 1941 - First African-American surgeon selected to serve as an examiner on the American Board of Surgery.
  • 1944 - Awarded Spingarn Medal by NAACP
  • 1950 - Died at the age of 45 due to injuries resulting from a car accident
“Excellence of performance will transcend artificial barriers created by man.” — Dr. Charles Drew
